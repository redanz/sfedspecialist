My Approach

Every learner has a unique profile of strengths and challenges. I help students understand how they learn best and build the tools they need to thrive in school and beyond.

Whole-child, strength-based support

Sessions blend academic skill-building, executive function coaching, and emotional support. We focus on small, sustainable shifts that reduce frustration and build confidence over time.

I collaborate closely with parents, teachers, and other professionals to create a consistent, aligned plan that supports the student across home and school.

Services

Personalized support for the way your child learns.

Educational Therapy

One-on-one sessions focused on reading, writing, and math skills using evidence-based, multisensory strategies.

  • Support for dyslexia, dyscalculia, ADD/ADHD, and processing differences
  • Reading: decoding, fluency, and comprehension
  • Writing: organization, grammar, mechanics, and editing
  • Spelling and vocabulary development
  • Foundational math skills

Executive Function Coaching

Support for students who struggle with planning, organization, and follow-through, often related to ADHD.

  • Planning, time management, and breaking tasks into steps
  • Homework systems and daily routines that actually work at home
  • Study strategies, test prep, and project planning
  • Building motivation, confidence, and follow-through

Parent & School Collaboration

Guidance and advocacy to help families navigate school supports and build a team around the student.

  • Communication with teachers and school teams
  • IEP / 504 meeting preparation and participation (if applicable)
  • Helping families understand evaluations and recommendations
  • Administration & interpretation of Slingerland screening
  • Practical tools to support your child at home

FAQ

What ages do you work with?

I primarily work with students in grades 2–12. If you have questions about whether your child is a good fit, reach out and we can talk.

Do you offer online sessions?

Yes. Depending on the student’s needs, we can meet in person in San Francisco, online, or a blend of both.

How is educational therapy different from tutoring?

Tutoring focuses on subject content. Educational therapy focuses on the underlying learning processes, executive functioning, and long-term strategies so your child becomes more independent and confident in school.

Do you provide assessments?

I provide informal academic assessments to understand your child’s learning profile. If a full psychoeducational evaluation is needed, I can refer you to trusted neuropsychologists in the area.

How long does it usually take to see progress?

Most families notice changes in confidence and organization within a few weeks. Academic changes take place gradually and consistently over time.
